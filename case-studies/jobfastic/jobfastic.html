01 overview

Bridging the gap between blue-collar talent and top employers in Saudi Arabia

JobFastic is a modern job marketplace built specifically for the Saudi Arabian labor market. Unlike traditional job boards that cater to office workers, JobFastic targets a largely underserved segment: skilled blue-collar workers across industries like logistics, automotive, construction, and cleaning services etc.

The platform needed to work for both Arabic-speaking job seekers and English-speaking recruiters simultaneously — requiring careful bilingual UX design, culturally appropriate imagery, and an onboarding flow accessible to users with varying digital literacy levels.

02 The Problem

Blue-collar workers were invisible to digital hiring pipelines

  • 🔍 Fragmented Discovery Job seekers relied on WhatsApp groups and word-of-mouth — no structured search or filtering existed for their trade types.
  • 📄 No Standardized CV Flow Workers had no accessible way to present their skills. Most lacked formal CVs, leading to high drop-off in traditional platforms.
  • 🌐 Language Barriers Existing platforms were English-first, excluding Arabic speakers who make up the majority of the target user base.
  • 🏢 Recruiter Mismatch Employers posting on generic boards received irrelevant applications. No vertical focus meant low signal-to-noise for both sides.

04 Design Process

From chaos to clarity in 4 steps

A structured double-diamond process with heavy emphasis on validation at each gate.

01

Discovery & Research

5 user interviews, competitive analysis of 4 regional job platforms, and card sorting sessions to define the information architecture for both job seekers and recruiters.

02

Information Architecture

Defined dual-flow sitemaps — one for candidates (search → apply → track) and one for recruiters (post → manage → shortlist). Validated with tree testing.

03

Wireframing & Prototyping

Low-fi wireframes in Figma for 50+ core screens. Ran 3 rounds of usability testing on the search + apply flow. Iterated on the onboarding completion card based on feedback.

04

Visual Design & Handoff

Full design system: Gilroy typography, blue (#2D82FF) primary palette, component library with 100+ elements. Full handoff with auto-layout, spacing tokens, and developer notes.

RTL / LTR Toggle

Arabic speakers switch to RTL in one tap — the layout mirrors completely with no redesign required.

4-Step Onboarding Card

The progress indicator in the hero ('2 of 8 data') drives 34% higher profile completion vs. platforms without it.

Category-First Browse

Job seekers browse by trade type (Driver, Cleaner, Builder) before keyword search — reducing cognitive load and mismatch.

08 Key Learnings

Key Learnings

Results measured across a 4-weeks moderated usability study and post-launch analytics from the MVP beta.

  • → Progress indicators in onboarding drive significantly higher completion — even a minimal '2 of 8' label changed behavior.
  • → Arabic users preferred browsing by category over keyword search — vocabulary mismatches caused 30% of failed searches.
  • → Image-first job cards outperformed text-first cards in trust ratings by +22 points.
